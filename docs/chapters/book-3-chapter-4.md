# III.4: The Bedroom (pp. 555–590)

The fourth chapter of Book III returns us to the sleeping parents: HCE and ALP in their bed, moving toward dawn and the final ricorso.

## Synopsis

### The Bed of Trial (pp. 555–570)
HCE and ALP in bed—a "trial" of their marriage, with accusations and defenses exchanged.

### Memory and Desire (pp. 570–582)
The parents remember their past, their courtship, their life together.

### The Children (pp. 582–586)
The children are nearby, sleeping or half-waking.

### Approaching Dawn (pp. 586–590)
Morning approaches. The night's long dream nears its end.

---

## The Bed as Court

The bedroom becomes another courtroom:
- HCE faces accusations about his fall
- ALP plays accuser, defender, and mediator
- Their dialogue oscillates between tenderness and recrimination
- The "trial" of a long marriage

---

## Key Themes

### 1. Marital Intimacy
The chapter depicts the complex dynamics of a long marriage—grievances, affections, disappointments, and continuities.

### 2. Memory
The parents remember their younger selves, their courtship, their hopes.

### 3. The Body in Age
Sexual activity (or its memory) mingles with the realities of aging bodies.

### 4. Dawn Approaching
The transition from night to day—the ricorso is near.
