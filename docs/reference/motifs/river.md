# The River

The River is Anna Livia Plurabelle—the Liffey flowing through Dublin, all the rivers of the world, water as time, life, and renewal. Where HCE is fixed geography, ALP is flowing water.

## Anna Livia as the Liffey

ALP is identified with Dublin's River Liffey:

> "Anna was, Livia is, Plurabelle's to be."

The Liffey:
- Rises in the Wicklow Mountains
- Flows through Dublin
- Empties into Dublin Bay
- Returns as rain

This is ALP's journey through the book.

---

## Chapter I.8: Anna Livia Plurabelle

The most famous chapter, I.8 (pp. 196–216), is the river's defining passage:

### The Setting
- Two washerwomen on opposite banks of the Liffey
- They gossip about ALP
- Dusk falls
- They transform into tree and stone

### The River Names
Joyce weaves **500+ river names** from around the world into the text:

> "O tell me all about Anna Livia! I want to hear all about Anna Livia. Well, you know Anna Livia? Yes, of course, we all know Anna Livia. Tell me all. Tell me now."

Hidden in the prose: Seine, Amazon, Nile, Thames, Ganges, Mississippi...

### Joyce's Recording
This is the only chapter Joyce recorded himself reading—the **only audio** of Joyce performing *Finnegans Wake*.

## The River's Journey

The river traces ALP's life:

| Stage | River Image | Life Stage |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Source** | Mountain spring | Birth |
| **Youth** | Swift stream | Childhood |
| **Maturity** | Broad river | Marriage, motherhood |
| **Mouth** | Delta, estuary | Old age |
| **Sea** | Ocean | Death |
| **Rain** | Clouds return | Rebirth |

---

## The Water Cycle

The river embodies the **water cycle** as eternal return:

1. **River** flows to sea
2. **Sea** evaporates to clouds
3. **Clouds** return as rain
4. **Rain** feeds the river
5. Repeat forever

This mirrors:
- Vico's cycle of history
- The Wake's circular structure
- Life, death, rebirth

---

## River as Time

The river is **time itself**:

> "riverrun, past Eve and Adam's..."

### Heraclitus
> "You cannot step into the same river twice."

The river:
- Is always moving
- Is always the same river
- Connects past to present
- Carries everything away

### Duration
- HCE is **space** (fixed, geographical)
- ALP is **time** (flowing, durational)
- Together they make reality

## The 500 Rivers

Joyce embedded river names from every continent:

### European Rivers
- Liffey (Ireland)
- Seine (France)
- Thames (England)
- Danube (Central Europe)
- Rhine (Germany)

### World Rivers
- Nile (Egypt)
- Ganges (India)
- Amazon (South America)
- Mississippi (North America)
- Yellow River (China)

### How They Appear
> "beside the rivering waters of, hitherandthithering waters of. Night!"

"Hitherandthithering" conceals multiple river names.

## River and Cleansing

The washerwomen wash HCE's **dirty laundry**:

### Literal
- They clean clothes
- They scrub stains
- They gossip while working

### Metaphorical
- They air his sins
- They clean his reputation
- They carry away his guilt

The river **absolves** as it flows.

---

## The Final Monologue

Book IV (pp. 619–628) is ALP's river-voice:

### The Flow to Sea
> "I done me best when I was let. Thinking always if I go all goes."

ALP flows toward death (the sea):
- She remembers her life
- She tries to wake HCE
- She accepts mortality
- She promises return

### The Last Line
> "A way a lone a last a loved a long the"

The sentence doesn't end—it continues on page 3:
> "riverrun, past Eve and Adam's..."

The river is circular.

### Liquid Metaphors
- Speech as flowing

- Memory as river

## The Washerwomen's Transformation

At the end of I.8:

> "Can't hear with the waters of. The chittering waters of. Flittering bats, fieldmice bawk talk... My foos won't moos... I feel as old as yonder elm. A tale told of Shaun or Shem? All Livia's daughtersons. Dark hawks hear us."

One washerwoman becomes a **tree**, one becomes a **stone**:
- Shem and Shaun in female form
- Nature reclaiming the human
- Dusk ending the gossip
- The river flowing on without them

---

## River and Mother

The river is **motherhood**:

### Birth
- Water breaks before birth
- The child emerges from fluid
- The river is the birth canal

### Nurture
- The river feeds the land
- ALP gives "a gift to every citizen"
- Water sustains all life

### Return
- We return to water in death
- Burial at sea
- The cycle continues

## Reading the River

### Tips
1. **Listen for water** — Flow, rush, pour, rain
2. **Watch for river names** — They're everywhere in I.8
3. **Feel the rhythm** — ALP's prose flows musically
4. **Note the cycle** — Ending is beginning

### Key Passages
- **I.8 (pp. 196–216)**: The washerwomen chapter
- **IV (pp. 619–628)**: ALP's monologue
- **p. 3**: "riverrun" — the first word
