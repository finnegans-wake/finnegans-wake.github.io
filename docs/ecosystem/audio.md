# Audio Resources

*Finnegans Wake* was written to be heard. These audio resources bring the text to life.

## Video & Audio Book Project

**[onelittlegoat.org/finneganswake](https://www.onelittlegoat.org/finneganswake)**

A groundbreaking project to film and record all 17 chapters (~30 hours) of Finnegans Wake by May 4, 2029—the 90th anniversary of the novel's publication.

| Format | Filmed live performances with subtitles and page numbers |
| Duration | ~30 hours total when complete |
| Completion | May 4, 2029 (90th anniversary) |

## Complete Readings

### Patrick Healy (1992)
**Duration:** 19 hours 32 minutes
**Access:** [UbuWeb](https://ubuweb.com) (free)

Dublin actor Patrick Healy recorded the complete Wake over four days in 1992 at Bow Lane Studios, Dublin.

**Characteristics:**
- Fast-paced Dublin reading
- Strong Irish accent
- Captures the energy of the prose
- Good for getting the flow and rhythm

**Best For:** Readers who want to hear the Irish English; following along with the text; first exposure to the Wake's sound.

---

### Patrick Horgan (1985)
**Duration:** ~35 hours
**Access:** National Library Service recordings; Internet Archive

American actor Patrick Horgan's reading for the NLS (National Library Service for the Blind) is slower and more measured.

**Characteristics:**
- Careful enunciation
- Slower pace allows more comprehension
- Dramatic interpretation
- Complete and unabridged

**Best For:** Close study; readers who want to catch more detail; accessible pace.

---

### Naxos Audiobook
**Duration:** ~35 hours
**Access:** Audible, Naxos

Professional audiobook production with contemporary readers.

**Characteristics:**
- Modern recording quality
- Clear audio
- Available through standard audiobook platforms

## Live Performance

### Neal Kosaly-Meyer: Memorized Performance
**Location:** Seattle, WA
**Completion:** 2030 (projected)

Seattle composer and pianist Neal Kosaly-Meyer is memorizing and performing the entire Wake from memory—a 17-year project matching Joyce's composition time.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Pace | 37 pages per year |
| Method | One chapter learned and performed annually |
| Approach | Musical interpretation of the prose |
| Started | 2013 |
| Completion | 2030 |

**2025 Update:** Performing the "Tales From the Inn" chapter (December 2025).

Featured on the WAKE podcast discussing memorization technique and the musical qualities of Joyce's prose.

> "Finnegans Wake is a book to be experienced. It's a book to be heard. I think the beautiful thing about it is you can have a delightful time reading the book without understanding anything."
> — Neal Kosaly-Meyer

## Musical Adaptations

### Waywords and Meansigns
**[waywordsandmeansigns.com](https://waywordsandmeansigns.com)**
**Total Duration:** 70+ hours across three editions

An international collaborative project that set the complete Wake to music.

#### Editions

| Edition | Year | Contributors | Format |
|---------|------|--------------|--------|
| First Edition | 2015 | 17 artists | Complete text, one artist per chapter |
| Second Edition | 2016 | 17 artists | Complete text, different artists |
| Opendoor Edition | 2017– | 100+ artists | Shorter passages, ongoing |

**Latest Addition:** May 4, 2024 — "Waveleaplights" by Charlie O'Brien

**Characteristics:**
- Free download (Creative Commons)
- Experimental and varied musical styles
- Text is always audible and unabridged
- Over 300 contributors worldwide

**How to Contribute:** Submit recordings of short passages for the Opendoor Edition.

**Available at:** [waywordsandmeansigns.com](https://waywordsandmeansigns.com) and [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/waywordsandmeansigns)

## Historical Recordings

### James Joyce Reading ALP (1929)
**Duration:** ~8 minutes
**Access:** YouTube, British Library, various archives

Joyce himself recorded the "Anna Livia Plurabelle" section in 1929. This is the only recording of Joyce reading from the Wake.

**Why Essential:**
- Hear Joyce's intended pronunciation
- His pacing and emphasis
- The Irish cadences he heard
- Historical document

**Find it:** Search "Joyce Anna Livia Plurabelle recording"

## Using Audio Effectively

### Follow Along
1. Open the text
2. Play the audio
3. Follow with your eyes while listening
4. Stop to check annotations as needed

### Audio-First Approach
1. Listen to a section without the text
2. Let the sounds wash over you
3. Then read the same section
4. Compare impressions

### Study Sessions
1. Listen to a chapter
2. Re-listen with annotations open
3. Make notes on what you notice aurally
4. Discuss in a reading group
